General Usage and Connect/Disconnect Instructions

How To Connect

  1. Plug in the wind tunnel to the wall and to the computer's USB-C port.
  2. Open the 'Serial Port' drop down and take note of the COM port options.
  3. Locate the wind tunnel's on/off switch on the red base support of the tunnel (directly behind the test section) and flip it on.
  4. Click the 'Refresh' button.
  5. Open the 'Serial Port' drop down and select the COM port that was not there previously.
  6. Hit the 'Connect' button and the connection light should turn green shortly after.
    • If you select the incorrect COM port, you may have to kill the app in Task Manager and retry
  7. NOTE: You should hear the wind tunnel turn on and see the 'Measured Airspeed' go up to about 15 m/s and then work back down to 0 m/s.
  8. NOTE: If the tunnel does not connect, try to kill the app in Task Manager and retry.

Wind Tunnel Label

  • This is an editable space that can be altered to title the project as desired, but 'WIND TUNNEL' is the default text.
  • NOTE: Label options are limited by space.

Wind Tunnel Speed

  • If changing speed for the first time, wait for the tunnel to return to 0 m/s after start up before attempting to change speed.
  • Drag the white cloud graphic to the desired speed and hit 'SET SPEED'.
    • Blue Section - No AOA data for these speeds, so the Lift/Weight and Measured Angle of Attack features may not be available.
    • Green Section - AOA data is available and all features should operate.
    • Red Section - No AOA data, wind tunnel can still operate at these speeds but they are reaching the limits of the tunnel, so not all features are available.
  • Hitting the stop sign graphic will bring the tunnel speed back to 0 m/s and reset all graphics while keeping the tunnel connection.
  • NOTE: To bring the tunnel speed to 0 m/s please hit the stop sign instead of bringing the cloud slider back to zero and hitting 'SET SPEED' as this will make the tunnel run at full speed.

Measured Airspeed

  • NOTE: The measured airspeed value will be about 2-3 m/s off from the set wind tunnel speed, so keep in mind if trying to demonstrate AOA.

Lift and Weight Relationship

  • This feature works when the speed is within the green range, and is best demonstrated by adjusting the angle of attack of the pitot probe.
  • At an AOA of 30 degrees, the airplane will 'stall' and drop to the bottom of the screen.

Speed and Pressure Relationship

  • This feature works when the speed is within the green range, and is best demonstrated by adjusting the angle of attack of the pitot probe.

Measured Angle of Attack

  • This feature works when the speed is within the green range, and is best demonstrated by adjusting the angle of attack of the pitot probe.
    • Green Section - 'Mild' angle of attack
    • Yellow Section - 'Medium' angle of attack
    • Red Section - 'Harsh' angle of attack that will produce 'stall' in the lift/weight graphic.

How to Disconnect

  1. Press the stop sign button to drop the speed back to 0 m/s.
  2. Press the 'Disconnect' button and the connection light should turn red.
  3. Turn off the wind tunnel's on/off switch
  4. Unplug the wind tunnel from the wall and the computer's USB-C port.
