Feature Explanations and Functions

Wind Tunnel Label

  • This is an editable space that can be altered to title the interface as desired, with 'WIND TUNNEL' as the default text.
  • NOTE: Label options are limited by space.

Measured Airspeed

  • This feature should automatically update as the tunnel changes speed, increasing/decreasing the flame graphic respectively.

Lift and Weight Relationship

  • This feature is based on the pressure difference between the top and bottom alpha ports on the pitot tube.
  • The balloon represents the lift force caused by increased pressure on the underside of the plane.
  • The weight represents the force caused by pressure on the top of the plane.
  • The balloon and weight should scale appropriately to demonstrate that a positive AOA puts more pressure underneath the wing and causes it to lift, whereas a negative AOA puts more pressure on the top of the wing, causing it to fall.
  • If the AOA reads greater than 30 degrees, the airplane will 'stall' and drop down to the bottom of the screen.

Speed and Pressure Relationship

  • This feature demonstrates the inverse relationship between velocity and pressure on an airfoil, which is what makes a plane fly.
  • This feature reads the alpha port pressure difference and scales the arrows to represent the pressure and velocity vectors on the top and bottom of the airfoil.
  • The pink arrows represent pressure and the blue arrows represent velocity around the airfoil.
    • Positive AOAs will generate a larger pressure arrow underneath the airfoil, and a larger velocity across the top of the airfoil, shrinking the other arrows respectively.
    • Negative AOAs will generate a larger pressure arrow on top of the airfoil, and a larger velocity across the bottom of the airfoil, shrinking the other arrows respectively.
    • Zero AOA will return all arrows to their balanced positions, demonstrating steady, level flight.

Measured Angle of Attack

  • This feature is based on the difference in pressure read from the alpha ports on the pitot tube.
